## Abstract

The paper compares counties that won a large plant opening with runner-up counties that narrowly lost and uses incumbent manufacturers to estimate agglomeration spillovers.

## Core argument / findings
- Estimates causal spillovers by comparing "winner" and "loser" locations around discrete, high-stakes place-based shocks (large plant openings). Widely cited template for framing localized, timed interventions as quasi-experiments — the same logic underlying EDWC's staggered activation.
- The paper asks whether attracting a large manufacturing plant raises the productivity of incumbent plants nearby through agglomeration spillovers.
- Its main finding is that five years after a new plant opens, incumbent plants' total factor productivity is about 12% higher in winning counties than in runner-up counties.
- The spillover is stronger when incumbent plants share labour pools or technology links with the new plant, which pushes the interpretation toward local labour-market and knowledge-channel mechanisms rather than simple coincidence.
- The paper also shows that labour costs rise in winning counties, so the productivity gain is not equivalent to a one-for-one increase in profits.

## Method & data
- Paper type: empirical paper.
- Parsed coverage used here: pages 1-40 of 64 total pages, with direct PDF fallback for the core summary.
- The identification strategy uses "winner" and "loser" counties from Site Selection's large-plant siting competitions, then estimates augmented Cobb-Douglas production functions for incumbent manufacturing plants using Annual Survey of Manufactures data.
- The design is valuable for the thesis because it treats a geographically concentrated, time-specific infrastructure shock as a quasi-experiment while paying close attention to counterfactual selection.

## Caveats / limitations
- Use it to justify identification strategy or estimator choice, not as direct evidence on EDWC or China data centers.
- The setting is manufacturing agglomeration rather than energy or digital infrastructure.
- The winner-runner-up design is cleaner than the EDWC setting in some respects, so it is best used as a design precedent rather than a close contextual analogue.
