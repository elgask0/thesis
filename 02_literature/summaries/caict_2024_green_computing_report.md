## Abstract

Institutional report on how China is defining and evaluating "green computing" across equipment, facility efficiency, compute-energy coordination, and compute-use coordination, with hub-node case material tied to EDWC.

## Core argument / findings
- Primary Chinese institutional document organizing green computing around devices, facilities, compute-energy coordination, and regional hub development. Establishes that China already treats green compute as a multi-component measurement and governance problem — but has not built an integrated provincial CPC metric. Directly supports the measurement-side gap.
- The report asks how green computing should be defined and tracked in China as compute demand, AI workloads, and EDWC buildout expand.
- Its main conceptual move is the `ECCI` framework: efficient equipment, conservation at the facility layer, clean compute-energy coordination, and inclusive compute-use coordination.
- It provides concrete descriptive benchmarks for the Chinese context: by end-2023 China had 8.1 million active standard racks using roughly 150 billion kWh, equal to 1.6% of total national electricity use; reported data-centre carbon emissions were `0.84 亿吨`, roughly 84 million tonnes; and average PUE fell to 1.48 from 1.54 in 2022.
- The report also argues that the centre-west now matters for green-computing development: the share of national green-data-centre selections in central and western regions rose from 26.6% in the first batch to 46% by the fifth batch.

## Method & data
- Paper type: policy / technical report.
- Parsed coverage used here: pages 1-50 of 81 total pages, with direct PDF fallback for the summary sections.
- The report is descriptive and institutional. It synthesizes policy developments, industry practice, facility-efficiency indicators, renewable-power coordination, and hub-node case studies.
- Its measurement logic is multi-layered rather than causal: IT equipment efficiency, facility indicators such as PUE and WUE, energy-matching and green-power use, and sectoral application of compute.
