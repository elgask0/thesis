## Abstract

CAICT white paper building a province- and city-level computing-power index for China and reporting national compute-capacity totals, growth rates, and regional rankings.

## Core argument / findings
- Primary Chinese institutional document publishing a provincial-level computing-power development index together with national compute-capacity totals (2022: 180 EFlops basic-infrastructure scale, 302 EFlops total, 33% global share; 25 intelligent-compute centres in operation). Provides the quantified provincial compute-capacity context that complements CAICT 2024's green-compute framing — the 2023 report is the compute-scale/index anchor, the 2024 report the carbon/efficiency anchor. Supports the proposal's EDWC framing by giving quantified provincial compute concentration and growth rates prior to and during the go-live window.
- The paper asks how China's computing-power development should be measured across provinces and cities rather than described only with national totals.
- It builds the index around five dimensions: compute scale, compute industry, compute technology, compute environment, and compute application.
- The headline descriptive results are directly useful for the thesis: 2022 infrastructure-side compute scale reached 180 EFlops; total compute scale reached 302 EFlops with a 33% global share; 25 intelligent-computing centres were already in operation; and intelligent compute was growing much faster than the rest of the system.
- The provincial results matter for EDWC interpretation. The report shows strong concentration in the Beijing-Guangdong-Shanghai corridor and surrounding eastern provinces, while also noting rapid improvement in several western and interior provinces, including Guizhou, on the compute-scale index.
